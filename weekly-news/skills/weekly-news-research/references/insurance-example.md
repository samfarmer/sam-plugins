# Insurance Industry Weekly News Research - Complete Example

This document shows how the weekly news research methodology applies specifically to insurance industry news. Use this as a template for building your own topic-specific research framework.

## Scope Definition

**Focus**: U.S. insurance industry developments
**Lines of business**: Property & Casualty, Life & Health, Reinsurance
**Topics**: AI/technology, regulation, M&A, market trends, catastrophes, legal/compliance, product launches, personnel

## Impact Scoring Framework for Insurance

### 5-Dimension Scoring System (25 points maximum)

#### 1. Industry Impact (1-5 points)
Assesses breadth of effect across the insurance ecosystem

- **5 points** - Affects entire industry (all carriers, all lines)
- **4 points** - Affects multiple segments (P&C + Life, or carriers + distributors)
- **3 points** - Affects one major segment (all P&C carriers, or all agents/brokers)
- **2 points** - Affects sub-segment (personal lines only, or regional carriers)
- **1 point** - Affects single company or niche market

**Evaluation factors:**
- Number of carriers/brokers affected
- Market segments impacted (personal/commercial lines, life/health, reinsurance)
- Geographic scope (state ‚Üí regional ‚Üí national)
- Distribution channel impact (agents, brokers, direct, insurtech)
- Precedent-setting potential

#### 2. Financial Impact (1-5 points)
Measures effect on revenue, costs, and valuations

- **5 points** - Billions of dollars industry-wide, or >20% impact on affected companies
- **4 points** - Hundreds of millions industry-wide, or 10-20% impact
- **3 points** - Tens of millions industry-wide, or 5-10% impact
- **2 points** - Millions industry-wide, or 1-5% impact
- **1 point** - Minimal financial impact, or impact unclear

**Evaluation factors:**
- Premium revenue implications
- Claims cost effects
- Operational cost changes (efficiency gains or new expenses)
- Investment and valuation impacts
- Market capitalization changes

#### 3. Regulatory/Legal Impact (1-5 points)
Assesses compliance and legal significance

- **5 points** - Federal legislation or NAIC model law affecting all states
- **4 points** - Multi-state regulatory action or significant court precedent
- **3 points** - Single-state regulation with broad adoption potential, or major enforcement action
- **2 points** - State-specific regulation with limited scope, or routine compliance matter
- **1 point** - Guidance or interpretation with minimal enforcement implications

**Evaluation factors:**
- Compliance requirements changes
- Legal precedent implications
- Regulatory enforcement actions
- Legislative developments
- Multi-state coordination

#### 4. Technology/Innovation Impact (1-5 points)
Evaluates advancement in operations, products, or customer experience

- **5 points** - Industry-transforming technology (new business models, fundamental process changes)
- **4 points** - Significant advancement (major operational improvements, new product categories)
- **3 points** - Meaningful innovation (moderate efficiency gains, enhanced capabilities)
- **2 points** - Incremental improvement (minor enhancements, pilot programs)
- **1 point** - Minimal innovation (routine updates, limited scope)

**Evaluation factors:**
- AI and automation advancement
- Digital transformation progress
- Customer experience improvements
- Operational efficiency gains
- Insurtech developments and partnerships
- Data analytics and predictive modeling advances

#### 5. Timeline Impact (1-5 points)
Measures urgency of implementation or effect

- **5 points** - Immediate implementation required (compliance deadline within 30 days, urgent response needed)
- **4 points** - Near-term implementation (within 6 months)
- **3 points** - Medium-term implementation (6-12 months)
- **2 points** - Long-term implementation (1-2 years)
- **1 point** - Extended timeline (2+ years) or no action required

**Evaluation factors:**
- Regulatory compliance deadlines
- Competitive pressure timing
- Technology deployment schedules
- Market window of opportunity

### Scoring Examples

**Example 1: NAIC adopts new AI transparency requirements**
- Industry Impact: 5 (all carriers, all lines)
- Financial Impact: 3 (moderate compliance costs)
- Regulatory/Legal: 5 (model law, multi-state adoption expected)
- Technology/Innovation: 4 (significant AI governance changes)
- Timeline: 3 (12-18 month implementation period)
- **Total: 20/25** - Critical story, lead coverage

**Example 2: Regional carrier launches telematics product in one state**
- Industry Impact: 1 (single company, limited geography)
- Financial Impact: 1 (small revenue potential)
- Regulatory/Legal: 1 (routine product filing)
- Technology/Innovation: 2 (incremental use of existing tech)
- Timeline: 5 (immediate market availability)
- **Total: 10/25** - Brief mention or exclude

**Example 3: Major hurricane causes $15B in insured losses**
- Industry Impact: 4 (affects all P&C carriers with property exposure)
- Financial Impact: 5 (billions in claims, reinsurance impacts)
- Regulatory/Legal: 2 (standard claims handling, possible future regulatory attention)
- Technology/Innovation: 1 (routine claims technology use)
- Timeline: 5 (immediate claims surge)
- **Total: 17/25** - Major story, substantial coverage

## Special Considerations for Insurance

### Lines of Business Distinctions
Always specify which lines are affected:
- **Personal Lines** - Auto, homeowners, personal umbrella
- **Commercial Lines** - General liability, property, workers comp, commercial auto
- **Life & Health** - Life insurance, annuities, health insurance, disability
- **Reinsurance** - Treaty and facultative reinsurance impacts

### Geographic Specificity
Insurance regulation is state-based. Note:
- **State-specific** - Which state(s) are affected
- **Regional patterns** - Multi-state trends (e.g., coastal states, tornado alley)
- **National scope** - Federal involvement or NAIC coordination

### Carrier Size Categories
Impact varies by carrier type:
- **National carriers** - Top 25 by premium volume
- **Regional carriers** - Multi-state but not national presence
- **MGAs and program administrators** - Specialized markets
- **Insurtechs** - Tech-enabled carriers and platforms

### Distribution Channel Impact
Different impacts on:
- **Independent agents** - Multi-carrier distribution
- **Captive agents** - Single-carrier representation
- **Brokers** - Commercial and large account specialists
- **Direct writers** - Direct-to-consumer models
- **Digital platforms** - Online distribution

### AI Governance Focus Areas
Given regulatory attention to AI, pay special attention to:
- **Transparency** - Explainability and disclosure requirements
- **Bias and fairness** - Testing for discriminatory outcomes
- **Data privacy** - Use of consumer data in AI models
- **Vendor management** - Third-party AI tools and accountability
- **Model validation** - Testing and documentation requirements

## Search Strategy for Insurance

### Phase 1: Broad Searches (30 minutes)
- "insurance industry news" + date range
- "insurance regulation" + date range
- "insurance M&A" + date range
- "insurance AI" + date range
- "catastrophe insurance" + date range

### Phase 2: Specific Searches (45 minutes)
- Major carrier names + "news" + date range
- "NAIC" + date range
- "state insurance" + specific states + date range
- Insurtech company names + date range
- "insurance lawsuit" OR "insurance settlement" + date range

### Phase 3: Direct Source Review (45 minutes)
- Visit Insurance Journal, Business Insurance, Carrier Management
- Scan headlines from past week
- Check NAIC press releases
- Review major carrier earnings if released during the week

### Phase 4: Cross-Reference Check (30 minutes)
- Follow up on stories that reference related developments
- Search for additional coverage of high-impact stories
- Verify facts across multiple sources

## Typical Story Categories for Insurance

### High-Impact Categories (usually 15+ points)
- NAIC model laws and bulletins
- Federal legislation affecting insurance
- Major M&A (>$1B valuations)
- Significant catastrophe events
- Large-scale regulatory enforcement
- Industry-wide technology shifts

### Moderate Impact (10-14 points)
- State regulatory changes
- Mid-market M&A and partnerships
- Product launches by major carriers
- Technology implementations
- Regional catastrophe events
- Executive appointments at major carriers

### Lower Impact (5-9 points)
- Company-specific product updates
- Small M&A and partnerships
- Pilot programs and trials
- Routine regulatory filings
- Individual executive moves
- Minor legal settlements

## Weekly Report Structure for Insurance

### Standard Sections
1. **Top Stories** (20-25 points) - 2-3 stories, most comprehensive coverage
2. **Major Developments** (15-19 points) - 3-5 stories, substantial detail
3. **Notable News** (10-14 points) - 2-4 stories, concise coverage

### Optional Special Sections
- **AI & Technology Spotlight** - Most significant tech advancement of the week
- **Regulatory Watch** - Compilation of regulatory developments
- **Market Trends** - Cross-story pattern analysis
- **What to Watch** - Upcoming events and pending decisions
